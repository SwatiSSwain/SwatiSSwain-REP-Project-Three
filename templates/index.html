The Pursuit of Happyness


Background:

For 8 minutes and 46 seconds, Derek Chauvin pressed his knee into the neck of George Floyd, an use of force by the now-former Minneapolis police officer has reinvigorated a very public debate about police brutality and racism.

The very systems that have been built to improve the systems of the past remain oppressive in many ways. The police force and real estate are two examples of systems continuing to oppress people of color in particular. Living in Minneapolis through the murder of George Floyd was an experience that lead us to analyze these two systems in Minneapolis further. In this website, we analyze Minneapolis, its neighborhoods, its demographics and police use of force.

Project Objective:

To facilitate an analysis of the relationship between police incidents, the use of force by police in those incidents, and the demographic characteristics of the neighborhood in which the incidents occurred such as race and income.

Data Sources:

1. Police incident data by neighborhood - Minneapolis Open Data
2. Police “use of force” data by neighborhood - Minneapolis Open Data
3. Household Income levels by neighborhood - Web Scraping from MN Atlas
4. Race distribution by neighborhood - Web Scraping from MN Atlas
5. Geojsons for Minneapolis neighborhoods and police use of force

Data Munging Techniques:

1. ETL - Extracted, transformed and loaded data to Postgres via Pandas
2. Built as Flask app to pull data from database and render it to the website
3. Analysis of data in Python and R to run statistical tests and find corelations between different datasets
4. Visualization - Used a combination rest API, HTML/CSS, Javascript(D3.js, Leaflet, Plotly), and dynamic page rendering from Postgres
5. Deployment - Heroku was used to deploy the website
6. Additional js library: Slick was used to create the carosel in the home page
